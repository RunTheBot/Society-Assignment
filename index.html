Introduction

Internet censorship in schools is a controversial topic. It has to balance both student safety with access to information. Schools implement various filtering measures to protect students from inappropriate content, but these systems can sometimes impede legitimate educational pursuits.

This project examines the methods of internet censorship in schools, their effectiveness, and the broader implications for students' digital literacy and education.

Key Aspects Covered

  • Common censorship methods in educational settings
  • Educational impact of filtering systems
  • Ethical considerations of internet restrictions
  • Balanced approaches to internet access in schools
